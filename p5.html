Project #5: Exquisite Corpse:

Project #5 Exquisite Corpse

Date:

3/11/24

Medium:

P5 Code

Decription:

A mix of 3 sketches from Alannah, Chloe, and Aeir redesigned in code

Design Process:

While developing the sketch, I realized that I struggled a lot with creating shapes in p5js because I didn't know the exact X and Y coordinates to line shapes up. As a result, I searched for and imported in a grid that would allow me to see the x and y axis based on my mouse position. Through a mix of trial and error, curves, and functions, I slowly created the exquisite corpse.

My guy, Sam, was developed out of a personal craving for watermelon. His creation came from my desire to draw something related to horror. Thinking about how awful it'd be to suddenly turn into a food, Sam came into being.

Sketches:

Ally: Sam??? this is sam, a scientist working on developing sentient fruits. While he was experimenting on watermelons, he kept one aside for a snack not realizing it was one of his experiments. Later that night he turned into a half human half watermelon typa guy. It's not going so great on his end.

Alannah: This is Albert the evil pig became evil after he found out that his other pig friend was turned into bacon so he vowed to seek revenge.

Chloe: this is a magician that will be cut in to three-halfves because he is performing in a magic show. he is going to fail and become three parts so our project can work.

Aeir: This is a recreation of one of the Cathrine Opie's Portrait works. A representation of sexual minorities and their struggle. I'd like to call her Dyke.

Exquisite Corpse: Sam accepted his fate. While experimenting on himself, he opened a interdimensional wormhole that collided in on itself. He's in a sillier situation now, his conscious merged with Albert and the magician. They can't make out what's going on, overwhelmed by the constant noise of three simultaneous minds and thoughts. The only thing that they can come to a consensus on is revenge through whatever means necessary to revert themselves.

Reflection:

I really like the drawing aspect of this project. Until I realized we had to make our drawings out of code. Overall this was pretty fun though, it challenged me to work with new shapes and functions to a point where I've become uncomfortably comfortable with curves and custom shape creation. I enjoyed being able to develop a character and a story behind their development. :)
